Introduction & Objectives

Purpose

This partnership mapping provides a comprehensive visual and strategic overview of Papua New Guinea's education sector landscape. It identifies key stakeholders, investment flows, and collaborative mechanisms that support PNG's education transformation agenda.

The report is designed to strengthen coordination among development partners, eliminate duplication, and optimize the strategic alignment of international and domestic investments with national education priorities.

Background

This mapping exercise was initiated by the Project Management & Aid Coordination Division of the National Department of Education in response to the need for greater transparency and strategic oversight of partnership activities.

The effort reflects PNG's commitment to aid effectiveness principles and supports the implementation of the National Education Plan through more coordinated, evidence-informed approaches to education development.

What this report enables

Strengthen Coordination

Enables NDoE, bilateral and multilateral donors, implementing organisations, civil society, and private sector to align around shared priorities.

Eliminate Duplication

Make investment flows and program footprints transparent across all 22 provinces, so resources reach where they're most needed.

Align with National Plan

Anchored to the National Education Plan's focus areas and the Education Transformation Vision 2075.

Scope & Methods

Geographic Coverage

This partnership mapping encompasses Papua New Guinea's complete education ecosystem, capturing activities and investments across all 22 provinces and the National Capital District.

The comprehensive geographic coverage ensures no region is overlooked — from urban centres to the most remote rural communities — providing a complete picture of education development efforts nationwide.

Methods

The exercise used Kobotoolbox for digital data collection, administering structured surveys to education sector partners operating in Papua New Guinea to capture organisational and program information.

Responses were consolidated, cleaned and standardised, and validated by cross-referencing against known program information. Where gaps existed, supplementary information was gathered from partner websites, annual reports, and program documentation.
